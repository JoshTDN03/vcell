3.4 Fitting Spatial Model
A spatial simulation can be conducted after the bioModel has been generated. The simulation results are used to compare with the original FRAP experimental. There are data viewers in the “Fit Spatial Model” panel. The two viewers are,

  • The simulation results viewer - The simulation results show how the diffusion works in the cell with nine different rates. Those nine diffusion rates are shown in the “Variable” list. When users select one of the diffusion rates, the corresponding data set will be loaded in the viewer.

  • The original data and ROI rings viewer - The original data are saved in two forms which are raw data and normalized raw data. The normalized raw data is the proper dataset to compare with the simulation results because the simulation runs based on the normalized data. The ROI rings are sample at different distances from the user defined initial bleached region. The ROI rings enable users to observe how diffusion works in others regions of cell besides the bleached area.

To run spatial simulation, users just need to click on the “Fit Spatial Model” tab. When all the required data are ready, the simulation will complete automatically. (Figure 3-6 shows the spatial simulation results)

To understand and export the spatial simulation results. Please refer to Virtual Cell documentation at http://vcell.org/userdocs/Rel/user_guide.pdf for chapter 8 and chapter 9.
