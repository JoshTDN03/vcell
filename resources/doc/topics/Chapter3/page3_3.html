3.3 Generating Spatial Model
It is important to evaluate whether the estimated diffusion rate and mobile fraction fits well or not. In Virtual Frap, a Vitual Cell 2D bioModel can be created based on whole cell area ROI, diffusion rate and bleaching while monitoring rate (Figure 3-5 shows the generated spatial model).

To generate spatial model, users just need to click on the “Generate Spatial Model” tab. When all the required data are ready, the model will be generated automatically.

2D geometry - It is defined to have two volumes which are cytosol and extra cellular. Cytosol is based on the whole cell area. The rest area in the image belongs to extra cellular.

Species - Nine species are created with diffusion rates derived from the base estimated diffusion rate to perform comparison. Those derived diffusion rates are well distributed around the base estimated diffusion rate.

Initial Condition - The initial value for species is calculated as intensity of first post bleach under bleached area divided by the intensity of average of prebleach under bleached area.

Reactions - If the bleaching while monitoring rate is available, the species will set to be a reactant in a Mass Action reaction with forward rate equals to the bleaching while monitoring rate. Otherwise, there is not reactions.

Simulation - One simulation has been generated to simulate how the species diffuse (and react if there are reactions) in the cell.
